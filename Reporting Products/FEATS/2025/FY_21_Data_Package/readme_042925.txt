## Project Information

Project Name: Hood Canal and Admiralty Inlet Nearshore Assessment with focus on; Assessing Effectiveness of Proposed Mitigation Measures at the Hood Canal Bridge

Project Description: [To better understand the Hood Canal & Admiralty Inlet nearshore and marine systems as they relate 
to steelhead smolts, other ESA-listed juvenile salmonids, and forage fish with a focus on near-field fish behavior 
associated with the Hood Canal Bridge and Port Gamble Bay. The goals for this project are to characterize the 
presence and behavior of juvenile Steelhead, Chinook Salmon, Chum Salmon, forage fish, and their predators, 
within the vicinity of the Hood Canal Bridge. Methods used during this project will include visual acoustics data 
collection, predator behavior surveys, and remote underwater video collection, to provide quantitative data 
which will inform evaluation of the mitigation measures deployed at the Hood Canal Bridge. This project is part 
of a broader undertaking which sought to understand the fish passage impacts associated with the Hood Canal 
Bridge during Phase 1 (see the 2016 Hood Canal Bridge Ecosystem Impact Assessment Plan, for a description 
of how this project is nested within the broader undertaking) and seeks to investigate effectiveness of 
mitigation strategies in Phase 2. 

Successfully accomplishing the goals listed under our objective will: Provide the Port Gamble S’Klallam Tribe 
and our partnering organizations with additional tools and information necessary to evaluate mitigation 
measures to reduce mortality of juvenile salmonids at the Hood Canal Bridge, as well as prioritize restoration 
and conservation projects, monitor recovery effectiveness, and provide more accurate information to fisheries 
managers.]

Funding Source:Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)

Project Point of Contact: Hans Daubenberger

Relevant Acronyms: [TAST = Targeted Acoustic Startle Technology; FGS = Fish Guidance System; HCB = Hood Canal Bridge; EV = EchoView ]
